Chapter 1

Introduction to Electric Drives

Establishes the big picture: what an electric drive system is, why we need controlled drives, and how the motor, power converter, and controller work together. Sets the vocabulary and framework for everything that follows.

What You'll Learn

  • Components of an electric drive system
  • Classification of electric drives (AC, DC, stepper)
  • Choice of motor and converter for a given application
  • Operating quadrants and braking methods

Chapter 2

Dynamics of Electrical Drives

The mechanical heart of every drive: the torque equation. Three lectures progressively build from the fundamental equation of motion to handling real-world complexities like fluctuating loads, flywheels, hoists, and fans.

What You'll Learn

  • Fundamental torque equation: \( T - T_L = J \frac{d\omega}{dt} \)
  • Reducing multi-inertia systems to equivalent single-axis models
  • Sizing motors for fluctuating and cyclic loads
  • Flywheel moment of inertia calculation for load smoothing
  • Hoist drive dynamics with counterweight analysis
  • Fan and pump load torque-speed characteristics

Chapter 3

Electric Traction

Apply electric drive principles to transportation. Analyze the forces on a train, construct speed-time diagrams, compute energy consumption, and understand how regenerative braking recovers energy.

What You'll Learn

  • Types of electric traction systems (DC, single-phase AC, 3-phase AC)
  • Tractive effort, adhesion, and train resistance
  • Speed-time curves and their components
  • Specific energy consumption (SEC) derivation
  • Effect of gradients and curves on energy
  • Regenerative braking energy savings

Chapter 4

DC Motor Drives

The classic controlled drive: DC motors with power electronic converters. From single-phase SCR bridges to three-phase converters and modern chopper drives, learn to control speed and torque precisely.

What You'll Learn

  • DC motor equivalent circuit and torque-speed characteristics
  • Armature voltage control and field weakening
  • Single-phase half-wave and full-wave converter drives
  • Three-phase fully controlled converter drives
  • Chopper-fed DC drives and duty ratio control
  • Four-quadrant operation with dual converters

Quick Reference

Key Formulas at a Glance

The essential equations from all eight lectures, organized by topic.

Drive Dynamics (Chapter 2)

Fundamental Torque Eq. \( T - T_L = J \frac{d\omega}{dt} \)
Equivalent Inertia \( J_{eq} = J_m + \sum J_i \left(\frac{\omega_i}{\omega_m}\right)^2 \)
Equivalent Torque \( T_{L,eq} = \sum T_{L,i} \cdot \frac{\omega_i}{\omega_m} \)

Electric Traction (Chapter 3)

Tractive Effort \( F_t = 277.8 \cdot M_e \cdot \alpha + 9.81 \cdot M \cdot G + r \cdot M \)
Specific Energy Consumption \( \text{SEC} = \frac{E_{in}}{M \cdot D} \)

DC Motor Drives (Chapter 4)

Motor Voltage Equation \( V_a = E_g + I_a R_a \)
Speed-Torque Relation \( \omega_m = \frac{V_a}{K\phi} - \frac{R_a \cdot T}{(K\phi)^2} \)
Chopper Avg. Voltage \( V_a = \delta \cdot V_s \)
1-Phase Full Conv. \( V_a = \frac{2V_m}{\pi} \cos\alpha \)
3-Phase Full Conv. \( V_a = \frac{3V_{m,LL}}{\pi} \cos\alpha \)
